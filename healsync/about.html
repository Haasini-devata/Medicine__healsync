Medicine is the science and practice of establishing the diagnosis, prognosis, treatment, and prevention of disease. Medicine encompasses a variety of health care practices evolved to maintain and restore health by the prevention and treatment of illness. Contemporary medicine applies biomedical sciences, biomedical research, genetics, and medical technology to diagnose, treat, and prevent injury and disease, typically through pharmaceuticals or surgery, but also through therapies as diverse as psychotherapy, external splints and traction, medical devices, biologics, and ionizing radiation, amongst others.

Medicines are substances used to prevent, treat, or manage diseases and health conditions. They come in various forms, including tablets, capsules, syrups, injections, and topical applications. Some medicines require a prescription, while others are available over the counter.

Medicines play a crucial role in improving health by:

  1. Preventing illnesses (e.g., vaccines)
  2. Treating infections (e.g., antibiotics)
  3. Managing chronic conditions (e.g., insulin for diabetes)
  4. Relieving symptoms (e.g., painkillers)

The World Health Organization emphasizes the importance of safe, effective, and quality medicines to ensure global health.
